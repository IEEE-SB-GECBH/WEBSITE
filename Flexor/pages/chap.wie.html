ABOUT THE AFFINITY GROUP



IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) is a global network of IEEE members and volunteers dedicated to promoting women engineers and scientists, and is indeed inspiring girls around the world to follow their academic interests and find careers in engineering and science. It is an affinity group of IEEE which since it’s inception has globally created a buzz that still drives it’s members into achieving greater goals.

The WIE AG of IEEE GECBH has been in existence ever since the inception of the SB. This group has been very active and has succeeded in conducting inter and intra college events abiding by the mission and vision of WIE.

Grants & Scholarships



Grants & Scholarships



  • IEEE Frances B. Hugle Scholarship:The IEEE Frances B. Hugle Scholarship was established to honor the memory of Frances B. Hugle, her many significant engineering accomplishments, and to help provide the resources for female engineers to follow in her footsteps. Annually, the scholarship will be presented to one female student who has completed two years of undergraduate study in an engineering curriculum at an ABET-accredited university or college within the United States.
  • Edith Hannigan McHale Scholarship: The Edith Hannigan McHale Scholarship may be awarded annually to a female student at John Adams High School, Long Island, New York, USA, who has enrolled in a major/program of study in a field of engineering, Physics, or math at an accredited four-year college or university.
  • 2U Scholarship Program:IEEE Women in Engineering is pleased to offer members opportunities for continued education. In partnership with the prestigious universities below, members have the ability to take advantage of tuition benefits up to $10,000. Please reach out to women@ieee.org subject line “2U Scholarship” to verify your eligibility.
  • Travel Grant: The IEEE WIE Travel Grant program provides grants for IEEE WIE members to attend and present at IEEE-sponsored conferences or IEEE WIE-sponsored events.
